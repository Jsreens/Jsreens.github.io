Hourly Graph of Arrest Outcomes by Denver Police in November

Take aways: After conducting a time/precinct analysis, which can be found here, I found that out of 6365 total incidents, only 4.264 percent were arrests. This is 985 total arrests in the month of November. I think this is a very interesting statistic to point out for a couple of reasons. Assuming that all non-arrests are non violent, it is safe to say that in the large majority of precincts, a police officer, but more spefically the multiple weapons they carry are unnecessary and a waste of resources to the city.

You can get a visual representation of this by looking at the stacked bar chart above. You can see that even during the busiest times of the day, the arrest count is proportionally small. This implies that a large number of interactions could be less reliant on power and intimidation.

While I think it's good that people aren't being arrested, this aggregation leads me to believe we have more interactions with the police than necessary.

On a separate note, another interesting observation bout this dataset is the dramatic increase in police precincts from 8 to 9 p.m. This is suprising to me because this seems fairly early in the evening. It's possible that this increase of numbers is due to more people being out during this time period.
